A Christian Community that welcomes all.

The House was established in 1961, by the Methodist Church, York and Hull District, as part of a chain of Methodist International Houses, which came into being because Miss Hilda Porter, at Mission House in London saw students in the 1950s from overseas on the streets of London and looking for lodgings.

Often they had to accept very poor quality apartments in inhospitable places. She saw their conditions and did a ‘small thing’ in inviting students to tea on Sundays at her apartment. She then spread the idea of offering weekend hospitality to overseas students through the Methodist districts.

From these small beginnings Districts raised funds to establish student residences usually known as “M.I.H” in each city. Hull International House (“H.I.H”) was a community-wide venture led by the Methodists but having support from other denominations and faith groups, and also with civic and University support.

It has tried to be ‘more than a roof’ to all who have been residents, with a chaplain and local support group “The Friends of HIH”. The management committee still reflects this wide base of support. Prudent management, loyal wardens and staff and the support from the District and “The Friends” mean that HIH remains open and serving present needs when other International Houses have closed.

Facilities that are available to you...

Each resident has a study bedroom, with single bed, desk, desk lamp, wardrobe and wash basin. Rooms are cleaned by domestic staff.

There is free provision of Internet, a computer room with wired access on the top floor and wireless access in every student room. Your fee includes payment for the utilities, gas, electricity and water. There is a laundrette. Payment for tokens to operate the machines, including a dryer is the only extra cost. There is a Lounge and games room with snooker and table tennis and TV. There is an additional TV room. There are shared Kitchens, recently renovated to the required standard for the licensing of the House by Hull City Council. There toilets, showers and bathrooms, again as regulated for the licensing of the House.

A deposit of £100 is required to secure a room. This is usually payable by cash or cheque. If you wish to make a booking or need further details, please contact the warden in person, by email or telephone.

Daily Prayers

Resident led daily prayers are held each day in social areas. A prayer room is available for individuals or group prayer sessions.

Bible Studies

Weekly Bible studies are held at the house by 'friends of the house', hosted by Christian Community members.

Alpha Courses

In association with 'fiends of the house' we run a weekly Alpha course, to introduce residents and non-residents to Christianity.

Holy Spirit & Healing

We are currently hosting a weekly course on the Holy Spirit and Spiritual Healing in order to trial it for the wider faith community in the UK.

Triathlons

Every month we hold and in-house triathlon (featuring Snooker, Darts and Table Tennis). Prizes for winning include hand-painted pictures or sketched from the leader of our Art Classes.

Art Classes

Weekly art classes are held in our social areas with painting, sketching and general art techniques included in the course.

Our house motto is (whatever you need) - No Problem!

Open Doors & Street Angels

Twice a week a varid collection of housemates help out at the soup kitchen, which is run by Open Doors, and with the Street Angels.

Selby Street Mission

We regularly send teams of helpers to Selby Street Methodist Church, to help with a wide range of activities including their soup kitchen on Tuesdays.

Anlaby Park Methodist Church

As well as worshipping here on a Sunday evening, our housemates are getting involved in Church ativities and helping when and where we can.

A Year of Prayer

As well as promoting and supporting the Year of Prayer for Hull Campaign, we will be taking part in praying throughout 2017.
